Security advisories are the public notices Nito publishes about security issues that affect the service: what the issue was, who was affected, what the impact was, and what has been done to remediate it. They give customers a reliable, dated record they can act on and reference in their own reviews.

<Warning>
No advisory process, advisory content, or notification channel is established in the codebase these docs are built from. This page describes the intended structure only. Advisory publication is owned by Security.
</Warning>

## What an advisory is expected to contain

Once the process is defined by Security, each advisory is expected to include:

- **Identifier and date.** A stable reference and the publication and last-updated dates.
- **Severity.** A rating and the method used to assign it.
- **Affected scope.** The components, versions, or surfaces involved.
- **Summary and impact.** What the issue was and what it could affect, in plain terms.
- **Remediation and status.** The fix or mitigation, and whether action is required by customers.
- **Timeline.** When the issue was discovered, disclosed, and resolved.

## How advisories relate to disclosure

Advisories are the outbound side of security communication. The inbound side, how someone reports a vulnerability to Nito in the first place, is covered separately on the [Responsible disclosure](/resources/trust-center/responsible-disclosure) page. A published advisory is often the eventual result of a report received through that channel.

## Where advisories will be published

The location, subscription mechanism, and cadence for advisories are not yet decided. Until Security defines them, no advisory feed should be assumed to exist, and no past advisory should be implied.
