Responsible disclosure describes how a security researcher can report a vulnerability to Nito safely and how Nito will respond. A clear policy protects users by getting issues fixed before they are exploited, and protects good-faith researchers by telling them what is in scope and what conduct is permitted.

<Warning>
  The reporting contact, encryption key, and the specific terms below are not established in the codebase these docs are built from. The structure is outlined so the final policy has a home, but no contact detail or safe-harbor promise here is binding until Security publishes it.
</Warning>

## Standard Structure

A responsible-disclosure policy typically has these sections. Nito's final policy is expected to follow the same shape:

- **Scope.** Which systems, domains, and surfaces are eligible for testing, and which are explicitly out of scope.
- **How to report.** The reporting channel, the encryption key for sensitive details, and what to include (steps to reproduce, impact, affected surface).
- **What to expect.** Acknowledgement and response timelines, and how status updates are communicated.
- **Rules of engagement.** Testing conduct that is permitted, and actions that are prohibited (for example, accessing other users' data, degrading service, or social engineering).
- **Safe harbor.** The commitment that good-faith research conducted within scope and rules will not lead to legal action.

## What is Not Defined Yet

The concrete details that make the policy usable, the reporting address, the PGP or other public key, the response-time commitments, and the exact safe-harbor language, are owned by Security and are not published here. Do not assume a reporting channel exists until it is listed.
