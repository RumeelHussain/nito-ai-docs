A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is the contract that governs how Nito processes customer data on your behalf. It defines the roles of the parties (controller and processor), the scope and purpose of processing, confidentiality and security obligations, subprocessor handling, data-subject request support, breach notification, and what happens to data at the end of the relationship.

<Warning>
  The DPA is a legal instrument owned by Legal. The terms are not established in the codebase these docs are built from, and no DPA language is authored or asserted here. Do not treat anything on this page as a binding term until Legal has published the final agreement.
</Warning>

## What the DPA is Expected to Cover

Once finalized by Legal, the agreement is expected to address, at minimum:

- Roles and responsibilities of the parties.
- Categories of data and data subjects, and the nature and purpose of processing.
- Security measures and confidentiality obligations.
- Use of subprocessors and the process for changes (see [Subprocessors](/resources/trust-center/subprocessors)).
- Support for data-subject rights and regulatory requests.
- Personal-data breach notification.
- Return or deletion of data on termination.

Each of these is drafted and approved by Legal, not inferred from system behavior. The verifiable engineering facts that a DPA narrative would reference (metadata-only default retention, provider-side no-retention enforcement) are documented under Privacy.
