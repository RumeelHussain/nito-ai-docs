The Trust Center is the front door for security, privacy, and legal artifacts about Nito (publicly Z Inference). It gathers the documents a reviewer, buyer, or compliance team asks for into one place, and points to where each one lives once it is published.

<Warning>
  Most artifacts linked below are owned by Legal and Security and are not yet published. Where a document is not final, the page for it is a stub that describes what it will contain and carries a clear TODO. Nothing here should be read as a compliance claim, a contractual commitment, or an SLA until the owning team signs off.
</Warning>

## What lives here

The Trust Center collects the following, each on its own page:

- [**Data Processing Agreement (DPA)**](/resources/trust-center/dpa) - the contractual terms that govern Nito's processing of customer data. This entry points to the existing [DPA page](/privacy/dpa).
- [**Subprocessors**](/resources/trust-center/subprocessors) - the authoritative list of third parties that may process customer data on Nito's behalf.
- [**Service Level Agreement (SLA)**](/resources/trust-center/sla) - availability and support commitments, once defined.
- [**Security advisories**](/resources/trust-center/security-advisories) - published notices about security issues and their remediation.
- [**Responsible disclosure**](/resources/trust-center/responsible-disclosure) - how to report a vulnerability to Nito safely.

## What is verifiable today

The engineering behavior a reviewer can check right now is documented under Privacy, not here. Start with the [Compliance posture](/privacy/compliance-posture) page, which draws a clean line between observable system behavior (metadata-only default retention, hashed credential storage, attested execution on the top tiers) and claims that require a legal or audit process. That page is the honest baseline any Trust Center artifact builds on.

## How to read this section

Treat each page as an inventory item, not a finished claim. Where a document exists, the page links to it. Where it does not, the page explains what the artifact is and marks the missing content as a TODO owned by Legal or Security. This keeps the gap between verified behavior and legal commitment visible on purpose.
