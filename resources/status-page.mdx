## What a status page will show

When published, a status page is expected to surface, at minimum:

- **Per-surface health** - separate indicators for the main surfaces, for example chat and text generation, embeddings, image generation, attachments and parsing, and authentication. The gateway already separates these internally, so per-surface reporting is the natural granularity.
- **Current incidents** - active degradations or outages with a short description and a running timeline of updates.
- **Scheduled maintenance** - upcoming windows announced ahead of time.
- **Historical uptime** - a rolling record of availability per surface.

## What it will not be

A status page reports availability. It is not an SLA and not a contractual commitment. Any availability target or credit is a separate legal artifact owned by Legal and Security.

## How to check status today

Until a status page exists, the observable signals are per-request:

- The HTTP status code and the error envelope (`message` plus `code`, no `type` field) tell you whether a specific call succeeded.
- The `Retry-After` header on a 429 tells you when to retry a rate-limited call.
- The `X-Request-ID` on every response is what you quote to support when reporting a problem.
