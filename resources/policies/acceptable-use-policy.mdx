The Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines what you may and may not do with Nito. It sets the boundaries on permitted use so that the service stays safe, lawful, and reliable for everyone, and it gives Nito a basis to act when use crosses those boundaries.

<Warning>
The terms of the Acceptable Use Policy are owned by Legal and are not authored in the codebase these docs are built from. This page describes what the AUP is and the categories it is expected to cover. It states no binding rule until Legal publishes the policy.
</Warning>

## What the AUP is expected to cover

Once authored by Legal, the AUP is expected to address, at minimum:

- **Prohibited content and activities.** Categories of use that are not permitted (for example, unlawful activity, abuse, or generating certain categories of harmful content).
- **Security and integrity.** Restrictions on probing, disrupting, or circumventing the service or its rate limits and access controls.
- **Fair use.** Expectations around automated access, volume, and resource consumption.
- **Third-party and provider terms.** The requirement to respect the terms of upstream providers in the inference path.
- **Consequences.** How violations are handled, including warning, suspension, or termination.

## How the AUP fits the other policies

The AUP is referenced by the [Terms of Service](/resources/policies/terms-of-service) and works alongside it: the Terms establish the overall agreement, and the AUP details the conduct rules. Enforcement mechanisms that exist in the system today (for example, rate limiting and access controls) are engineering behavior documented in the platform reference; they are not a substitute for the policy text, which Legal owns.
