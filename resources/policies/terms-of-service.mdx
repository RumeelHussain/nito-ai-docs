The Terms of Service are the agreement between you and Nito that governs your use of the service: the rights and responsibilities of each party, acceptable use, billing and payment terms, disclaimers and limitations of liability, and how the agreement changes or ends.

<Warning>
The legal text of the Terms is owned by Legal and is not authored in the codebase these docs are built from. This page describes where the Terms are served and what they cover in general terms. It is not the agreement itself, and nothing here is a term until Legal publishes it.
</Warning>

## Versioned terms served by the gateway

One thing is grounded in code: the gateway serves a versioned Terms page at the route **`/terms`**, and it carries a policy version value (`POLICY_TERMS_VERSION`) so that the terms in effect at a given time are identifiable. This means Nito serves terms at a stable route with an explicit version, even though the legal content of those terms is maintained by Legal rather than in these docs.

Referencing a specific version matters: when the Terms change, the version lets both parties point to exactly which text applied to a given period of use.

## What the Terms are expected to cover

Once authored by Legal, the Terms are expected to address, at minimum:

- Eligibility and account responsibilities.
- Acceptable use (see the [Acceptable Use Policy](/resources/policies/acceptable-use-policy)).
- Fees, billing, and payment.
- Intellectual property and license grants.
- Warranty disclaimers and limitation of liability.
- Suspension and termination.
- Governing law and dispute resolution.
- How changes to the Terms are made and communicated.

The [Privacy Policy](/resources/policies/privacy-policy) is a separate document, similarly served at a versioned route.
