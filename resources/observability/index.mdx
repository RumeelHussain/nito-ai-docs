Observability for Nito (publicly Z Inference) has two halves: what the gateway emits server-side, and what you capture client-side from every response. This section documents both honestly, and marks any third-party integration that is not verified in the gateway as a TODO owned by DevRel or Infra.

## What the gateway emits server-side

The gateway supports OpenTelemetry and Sentry through configuration:

- **OpenTelemetry** via `OTEL_ENABLED`, `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_ENDPOINT`, `OTEL_EXPORTER_OTLP_PROTOCOL`, and `OTEL_SERVICE_NAME`. Traces and metrics are exported over OTLP to any compatible collector.
- **Sentry** via `SENTRY_ENABLED` and `SENTRY_DSN` for server-side error tracking.
- **OpenObserve** via `OPENOBSERVE_*` configuration.

These are deployment-side settings. If you self-host or operate a Nito deployment, they control what the gateway ships to your telemetry backend. They are not per-request options a caller sets.

## What every response gives you

Regardless of server-side configuration, every gateway response returns two things you can log from the client:

- **`X-Request-ID`** - a request identifier, echoed from your `X-Request-ID` header if you send one (up to 128 characters, sanitized) or generated by the gateway otherwise. Log it so you can correlate a client-side event with a specific call.
- **A `usage` block** - `prompt_tokens`, `completion_tokens`, `total_tokens`, and optional detail fields such as `reasoning_tokens`, cached-token counts, and `server_tool_use`. Log it to track cost and volume.

This client-side pair is the most portable observability primitive Nito offers. It works no matter which backend you route to.
