Nito is the product: one key, many models, selectable privacy per call, and inference you can verify. Most of the time that is all you need to know. This page is for the moments when you want to understand what sits underneath.

## The Engine Behind Nito

Nito is powered by Z, the engine that provides the privacy and execution guarantees you use through the gateway. When you pick a privacy mode per call, or fetch an attestation and verify it yourself, those capabilities come from Z underneath. Nito is the clean, OpenAI-compatible surface on top of that engine.

We keep this reference discoverable rather than loud. In day-to-day use you work with Nito's API, models, and privacy modes, and you never have to think about the layer below. When you do want to look, this is the door.

## What This Means in Practice

The parts of Z you can already touch through Nito are the ones the gateway exposes directly:

- **Selectable privacy per call**, from ordinary routing up to TEE-sealed execution, chosen with a model-ID suffix. See [Per-call privacy modes](/privacy/per-call-privacy-modes).
- **Attested execution you can verify.** For TEE tiers, Nito returns raw attestation evidence so you can complete verification client-side. See [TEE attestation](/privacy/tee-attestation).
- **Metadata-only retention by default**, with prompt and completion contents treated as request-local. See [Zero Data Retention](/privacy/zero-data-retention).

These are Nito features first. That they are backed by Z is the framing, not a separate product you have to adopt.
