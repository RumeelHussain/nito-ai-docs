After your first successful call, the natural next question is: did that cost anything, and how much do I have left? Those are two questions with two sources. The **per-call cost** comes back in every API response, and your **balance and spend over time** live in your Nito dashboard.

## How Billing Works

Nito bills in credits. Internally those credits are tracked in nanos, billionths of a credit, so that fractions of a cent's worth of tokens can be accounted for exactly without rounding error. You will see `nanos` in the numbers; it is just the precise unit underneath the human-facing credit.

The flow around a single call has three steps:

1. **Hold.** Before spending real money on a provider call, Nito places a hold against your balance, sized to the expected cost of the request. A hold is like the pre-authorization a hotel puts on your card at check-in: it reserves funds without charging them yet, which guarantees you can pay for the work before it starts.
2. **Settle.** When the call succeeds, the hold is captured for the actual usage, which may be less than the estimate. You are charged what the call really used.
3. **Release.** If the request fails before producing any usage, the hold is released and you are not charged. This is exactly why a failed call does not cost you credits.

## What a Call Costs: The `usage`Block

Every successful chat response includes a `usage` block. This is your per-call record, available directly from the API with your `z_sk_` key — no extra request needed.

```json
"usage": { "prompt_tokens": 28, "completion_tokens": 64, "total_tokens": 92 }
```

`prompt_tokens` is input, `completion_tokens` is output, and `total_tokens` is the sum. Reasoning models also return `reasoning_tokens`, and when prompt caching applies you will see `cache_read_input_tokens` and `cache_write_input_tokens`. To turn tokens into a cost estimate, combine `usage` with the model's `pricing` object from `GET /v1/models`. See [Pricing](/models/pricing).

## Balance and Spend Over Time: Your Dashboard

Your credit balance and aggregated usage — totals, a daily breakdown, and a per-model breakdown — live in your **Nito dashboard**. These are organization-level views: billing in Nito is owned by the organization (the account that owns billing and keys), not the individual key.

<Note>
  Balance and usage-overview reads are authenticated with your **browser session**, not a `z_sk_` API key. Your API key is for inference; account billing views are read from the dashboard. There is no API-key-authenticated balance or usage-summary endpoint today.
</Note>

Balance is tracked in nanos (billionths of a credit); the dashboard converts it for display. If you ever handle a raw nanos value, divide by 1,000,000,000 (1e9) for whole credits — for example `4250000000` is 4.25 credits.

## Failed-request billing

The hold-then-settle design above is what makes failed-request billing intuitive:

- If a call errors before the provider returns any usage, Nito voids the hold and you are not charged.
- If a streaming call fails after some usage has already been recorded, the charge is reconciled for the usage that actually occurred rather than dropped. You pay for what was produced, not for the part that failed.

See [Failed request billing behavior](/api-reference/usage-and-credits/failed-request-billing).

## Common Issues

- **Trying to read balance or usage with your API key.** The org balance and usage-overview endpoints are browser-session authenticated and reject a `z_sk_` key. Read them from the dashboard; read per-call cost from the response `usage` block.
- **Treating nanos as whole credits.** Nanos are billionths. Divide by 1,000,000,000 (1e9) before showing a value to a person.
- **Expecting a real-time per-request ledger.** Use the response `usage` block for each call and the dashboard for aggregates. There is no public per-transaction history endpoint.

## Important Notes

- Retention: usage aggregates are derived from billing records, not from your prompt contents. Reading usage does not expose what was in any prompt.
