description: "Privately served models with zero-data-retention enforced upstream, so the provider is told not to keep your request."

T2 Private is the second rung of the [privacy ladder](/privacy/tiers). In the code it is the stored tier `private`. It keeps the metadata-only posture of [T1](/privacy/tiers/t1-anonymous) and adds one thing on top: the upstream provider is held to **zero-data-retention**, so it is told, contractually and technically, not to keep your request after serving it. Choose T2 when you want assurance about the provider's own retention, but do not need the sealed, attested execution of the TEE tiers.

## What it is

A T2 model is one the catalog reports with `privacy_tier: "private"`. These are privately served models: the gateway routes them through backends configured so that the upstream does not retain the data. When the router places a request on a private route, it sets a zero-data-retention requirement on the call, and that requirement is what the upstream backend is asked to honor.

The clearest example is OpenRouter. There, models from the major labs (Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI owners) are stored as `anon`, while other models routed through zero-data-retention endpoints are stored as `private`. So whether a given OpenRouter model lands you in T1 or T2 depends on the model, which is exactly why you read the catalog entry rather than assuming.

## Zero-data-retention, defined

**Zero-data-retention (ZDR)** means the upstream provider is instructed not to keep the request after it has served it. On a private route the gateway sets that requirement explicitly; for OpenRouter, the requirement is what drives selection of a ZDR endpoint, and models that cannot meet it are not imported into the catalog at all. The result is a stronger statement than T1's metadata-only default: not only does the gateway not persist your prompt and reply, the provider serving the call is held to the same.

What ZDR does **not** give you is a hardware-backed proof. T2 is an enforced requirement on the provider, not a sealed environment you can attest. If you need to verify where and how your call ran, that is [T3](/privacy/tiers/t3-private-plus-tee), where you can fetch and check an attestation.

## What it protects

- **No upstream retention.** The provider is told not to keep the request, on top of the gateway's own metadata-only default.
- **Inherits T1.** Everything T1 gives you (metadata-only retention, `no-store` responses, client-side chat history) still holds.
- **Privately served routing.** The call is placed on a backend configured for the private tier rather than ordinary anonymized routing.

## How to select it

You do not select T2 with a suffix. T2 is determined by the model's stored tier: name a `private`-tier model with no suffix and you are served in T2.

```bash
curl https://YOUR-NITO-BASE-URL/v1/chat/completions \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $NITO_API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "model": "MODEL-ID",
    "messages": [{"role": "user", "content": "Draft a reply to this internal email."}]
  }'
```

To know whether a model is private, read its catalog entry and look at `privacy_tier`:

```bash
curl https://YOUR-NITO-BASE-URL/v1/models \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $NITO_API_KEY"
```

If `privacy_tier` reads `private`, a suffix-free call gives you T2. If it reads `anon`, the same call gives you [T1](/privacy/tiers/t1-anonymous) instead. As with every tier, the suffix-free distinction between T1 and T2 is a property of the model, not something you toggle in the request body.

## When to choose it

Choose T2 for moderately sensitive work where the deciding factor is the provider's retention: confidential business content, customer data you do not want a provider to retain, or anything where "the upstream keeps nothing" is the assurance you need. If the call additionally needs a verifiable execution boundary, climb to [T3](/privacy/tiers/t3-private-plus-tee). If the model you want is only available at `anon`, you stay at T1; a privacy mode never silently upgrades or downgrades to a tier the model does not support.
