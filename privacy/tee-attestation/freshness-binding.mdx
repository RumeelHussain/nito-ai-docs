description: How report_data = signing_address + nonce is recomputed and compared, why anti-replay is caller-driven, and why reports are never persisted.
---

An attestation is only meaningful if it was produced for *your* request and not replayed from an earlier one. The mechanism that ties a report to a specific request is the **report-data binding**, and the freshness it provides is something you create by sending a fresh nonce. This page explains the binding, how the gateway checks it, and exactly where the responsibility for replay protection sits.

## The binding: `report_data = signing_address + nonce`

Inside a TEE, the hardware can stamp a small piece of caller-supplied data into the signed report. That field is the **report data**. Nito uses it to commit the report to two things at once: a signing identity and a nonce.

The binding is:

```
report_data = signing_address + nonce
```

The TEE binds this value into the report it signs. Because the value includes your nonce, a report produced for one request cannot stand in for another: a different nonce produces a different `report_data`, and the signature would not match.

## How the gateway checks the binding

The gateway does two separate things, and it is worth keeping them distinct.

1. **It recomputes the binding.** The gateway takes the signing address and the nonce, concatenates them in the same `signing_address + nonce` form, and compares the result against the `report_data` observed in the evidence. The comparison is **case-insensitive**. If the recomputed value does not match what the report committed to, the binding check fails.

2. **It requires the observed nonce to equal the caller nonce.** Separately from recomputing `report_data`, the gateway checks that the nonce observed in the provider's evidence equals the nonce you supplied as the caller. This is a distinct check: one confirms the report committed to `signing_address + nonce`, the other confirms that the nonce in play is the one you actually sent.

Both must hold. Together they establish that the report was produced for a request carrying your nonce and committed to the expected signing address.

## Anti-replay is caller-driven

Here is the part a security reviewer must not miss. **There is no server-side nonce store.** The gateway does not remember nonces it has seen, and it does not reject a nonce for having been used before. The freshness guarantee comes entirely from you choosing a fresh, unpredictable nonce on every request.

The consequences:

- **Send a fresh random nonce every time** and the binding checks confirm the report is fresh for this request. This is the supported, secure pattern.
- **Omit the nonce** and an **empty-binding report is permitted.** The endpoint allows it, but you get no replay protection from it. An empty binding commits to nothing fresh, so an old report could satisfy it.

So replay protection on Nito is not an unconditional property of the endpoint. It is a property of the protocol you follow as the caller. Do not describe a Nito attestation as replay-proof in the abstract; it is replay-proof only when you supply a fresh nonce and verify it was echoed back.

A practical recommendation: generate the nonce from a cryptographically secure random source, at least 32 bytes, encoded as hex, and never reuse one. The [verify-yourself recipe](/privacy/tee-attestation/verify-yourself-recipe) does exactly this as its first step.

## Reports are request-local

Attestation reports are **request-local**. They are produced for the request that asked for them and are **never persisted** by the gateway. The response carries `Cache-Control: no-store`, and there is no stored history of past reports. Each call stands alone: a fresh nonce, a fresh report, a fresh binding check. This is consistent with Nito's metadata-only default posture described in the [privacy overview](/privacy/overview).

## How this fits the baseline

The binding and the nonce match are gates 4 and 5 of the [seven-gate baseline](/privacy/tee-attestation/seven-gate-baseline), and gate 7 extends the nonce check into the nested payload for GPU envelopes. The address side of the binding is governed by an additional invariant covered in [address-only model binding](/privacy/tee-attestation/address-only-model-binding).
